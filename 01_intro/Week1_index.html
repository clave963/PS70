<div class="textcontainer"> <p class="margin"> </p> <h3>Week 1: Final Project Proposal</h3> <p class="margin"> </p> <p> For my Final Project in PS70 I'm interested in creating an LED light sculpture that expresses human body movement through a kinetic volumetric light design. This volumetric sculpture would take inputs of human body movement via a webcam or accelerometer and output them onto an LED grid of lights addressed through microcontrollers in a color gradient. The movements of the body would be translated to various color patterns and arrangements. For the initial prototype, this sculpture would be a small handheld model in size. <br> As I do not have prior experience in microcontrollers or light design, I imagine there will be a learning curve in programming light sequences and fabricating the LEDs. </p> <b> What *I think* this involve: </b> <p class="margin"> </p> <p> -programmable interface for light design <br> -LED strips sautered and organized into some 2D or 3D grid <br> -webcam input that translates body movement to the LEDs </p> <em>Project Idea</em> <p class="margin"> </p> <em>References and Inspiration</em> </div>
